Browser-based image wobble animation

purupuru maker: free image wobble maker

purupuru maker lets you upload an illustration, paint the parts that should move, preview a soft jelly motion, and export a short MP4 or GIF animation. Your image stays in your browser while you work.

How it works

Upload a PNG, JPEG, or WebP image

Start with a PNG, JPEG, or WebP file. The editor opens it locally and prepares a flexible mesh for motion preview.

Paint the motion mask

Brush over hair, clothing, stickers, avatars, or other details that should bounce while the rest of the picture stays steadier.

Preview and export

Tune strength, speed, spring, damping, and Motion over time, then export a short MP4 or GIF video directly from the canvas.

purupuru maker features

The tool is built for creators who want quick image-to-animation results without installing desktop software or uploading artwork to a remote editor.

  • Local browser processing for private image editing
  • Brush-based motion masking for selective wobble effects
  • Real-time preview powered by WebGL mesh deformation
  • Adjustable bounce, speed, spring, damping, and motion-over-time controls
  • MP4 and GIF export for social clips, stickers, memes, and avatars

FAQ

What is purupuru maker?

purupuru maker turns still images into soft jelly-like wobble clips. This tool is inspired by that playful effect and is built for character art, avatars, stickers, memes, and other images with details you want to make bounce.

How to use purupuru maker?

Upload an image, choose a brush thickness, paint the parts that should move, adjust strength, speed, spring, damping, and Motion over time, preview the wobble, then export the result as an MP4 video or GIF animation.

Does purupuru maker upload my image?

No. Image loading, mask painting, animation preview, and export run in your browser. The app does not upload your artwork to a purupuru maker server.

Which export format is supported?

The current editor exports MP4 or GIF when your browser supports canvas recording. Chrome and Edge usually provide the best support.

What images work best?

Illustrations, avatars, stickers, character art, and product images with clear movable details usually produce the cleanest wobble effect.
